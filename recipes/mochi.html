Mochi Ice Cream

Mochi ice cream is a small, round confection consisting of a soft, pounded sticky rice dumpling (mochi) formed around an ice cream filling.

The traditional ice cream flavors used are vanilla, chocolate and strawberry.

Ingredients

  • Shiratamako flour
  • Sugar
  • Powdered sugar
  • Cornstarch
  • Water

How to make Mochi

  1. Place parchment paper on a cookie sheet. Scoop and place ice cream balls on top. Freeze for 1 hour.
  2. In a microwave safe bowl mix flour, sugar, and powdered sugar until smooth. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for 1 minute. Use a wet spatula to fold the mixture afterwards. Microwave for 1 minute and fold the dough again. Microwave for 30 more seconds.
  3. Place the mochi dough on parchment paper with a layer of cornstarch. Dust the top of the dough ball with cornstach. Roll the mochi into a 1/4 inch thick rectangle. Refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the dough from the fridge and cut circles in it. Brush off the cornstach from top of the circles. Place 1 ice cream scoop inside and gently press the dough around theice cream. Pinch the edges to seal it.Wrap each mochi in a piece of plastic wrap and twist it tightly at the top.
  5. Freeze the mochi for at least 1 hour. Allow the dough to thaw slightly before eating.
